Image Map and Hotspot Tutorial

 

Before we move to the Evented UI series, we will need to understand Image Maps and Hotspots. They are one of the ways for you to create your own customized GUI.

 

 

 

What is an Image Map?

An Image Map is an image with clickable areas, known as Hotspots, that are triggered depending on the assigned state. To learn of the function of its each specific command, the link provides a quick overview of their functions.

 

Preparing your Graphics

We need to prepare two instances of our image map. It's important to remember that both versions of the image must be at the same size.

 

  • Ground - This image is the 'idle' state of your image map.

  • Hover - This image is the 'activated' state of your image map. This is what the image would look like if you hover or click one of its Hotspots.

Setting up the Image Map

Now that we have prepared the resources, it's time to add them in the Scene Content!

 

  • Create a new Scene.
  • Click the Picture -> Show Image Map.

 

  • As stated earlier, just set the images on their respective Ground and Hover locations.
  • OPTIONAL: Click the arrow and select Animation -> Blending so the image map fades in.

 

Setting up the Hotspots

  • Click the Hotspots button.
  • A window will appear. Press Add Hotspot. You will notice that a small box will appear. If your background is too dark, just remember that a Hotspot will always spawn at the top left.
  • Now just set your Hotspots. Afterwards, either Double Click or Right Click -> Properties the box. You will see a small window called Hotspot Properties. Here's a video to show how to do so:

  • Click the button for Jump To and set a label. For mine, I used "ask" and "leave" respectively. It would look like this:
