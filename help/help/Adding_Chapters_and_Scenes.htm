Adding Chapters and Scenes

 

Adding Chapters or Scenes is extremely easy. You can think of Chapters as Folders for Scenes.

 

It's important to see  we need to breakdown our story into chunks. To understand what I mean, we'll be using Little Red Riding Hood as case study and the Three Act Structure. We're not going to make a Little Red Riding Hood game, this is just to explain how to breakdown a simple story.

 

 

ACT ONE: INCIDING INCENT

  • Scene 1: My Little Village

  • Scene 2: Introducing Red and the Huntsman.

  • Scene 3: Red's Mission.

  • Scene 4: The Path to the Woods

  • Scene 5: Meet the Wolf

  • Scene 6 -- (LOCK IN): The Wolf visits Grandma

 

ACT TWO: CONFRONTATION

  • Scene 7: The Wolf attacks Grandma.

  • Scene 8 -- (MIDPOINT): Wolf in Grandma's Clothing and monologues his plan.

  • Scene 9: The Little Red finally arrives and describes Grandma's new features.

  • Scene 10 -- (MAIN CULMINATION): The Wolf attacks Red!

 

ACT THREE: RESOLUTION

  • Scene 11: The Huntsman arrives.

  • Scene 12 -- (CLIMAX): The Huntsman fights the Wolf.

  • Scene 13: The Huntsman succeeds in saving Red and they both return home.

 

And if you want to follow the Five Key Moments to the tee:

  • Scene 14 -- (THIRD ACT TWIST): The Wolf has a son that vows revenge against the huntsman.

 

  • Scene 15: The Moral of the Story.

 

You might be wondering why there are multiple scenes per chapter. This is for two main reasons:

  • You changed your mind and want to reorder the structure of the story. Breaking them down to scenes like this will make that easier.

  • It will be easier to fix issues and/or locate bugs.

 

Now that we have a clear plot outline, we can now proceed to the next step!

Creating Chapters

First we will create a Chapter. You can think of it as a folder that contains all the scenes together. While they are not necessary, they will keep your project organized!

 

  • Right Click on the Scenes Tab and press New Chapter.

 

  • Once a chapter is created, simply press the 'New Chapter' and rename the chapter inside the Name box.

 

  • By the end of this, the project will look like something like this:

As you can see, I added an extra chapter called, Test Bed. The purpose of this chapter is to insert scenes where they are meant to test out certain functions without ruining all the other final scenes or clutter the important chapters with useless scenes. You can also think of it as a Sandbox, Debug Room or Developer's Playground. It's a good practice for organization purposes!

 

Getting Started: Creating Scenes

Now that we have created chapters, it's time for us to insert Scenes! This is where most of the actions of your game is happening.  You cannot create Scenes unless there is a Chapter.

 

  • Select a Chapter.

  • Right Click on the Chapter and New Scene.

  • You will see a window called Scene Properties. Simply read the page for all the information about the window.

  • After creating your scenes, you will end up with something like this.

 

  • Now we're ready to start adding characters and the like! You can check our Importing Resources tutorial to import your own custom content.
